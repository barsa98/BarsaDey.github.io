The Human Effect: How One Species Amplified Life’s Diversity

I have always been drawn to the question of how life around a single species became so profoundly diverse across landscapes, histories, and minds. Among the countless species shaped by evolution, humans emerged as just one branch of the tree of life. Yet somehow, this one species became the rule-maker not only for itself, but for all others. We call the lion the king of the forest, but it is humans who decide which forests remain, which disappear, and where even kings are allowed to survive. I am fascinated by the quiet forces that shape who we become evolution, memory, environment, culture, faith, and time. These unseen currents shape not only our biology, but our societies, our power structures, and the stories we tell about ourselves.
